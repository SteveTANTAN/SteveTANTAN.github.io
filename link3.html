About

Hi, I am Xingyu Tan (谭星雨), currently a Ph.D. candidate in the Data and Knowledge Research Group at the School of Computer Science and Engineering, UNSW, and the Software Systems Group at Data61, CSIRO.

I am jointly supervised by Dr. Xiaoyang Wang, Prof. Wenjie Zhang, Dr. Qing Liu, Prof. Xiwei Xu, Dr. Xin Yuan, and Prof. Liming Zhu.

Previously, I completed both my Bachelor of Science and Master of Philosophy in Computer Science and Engineering at UNSW. My recent work studies graph-based reasoning, structured retrieval, temporal and memory-enhanced methods, and privacy-aware systems for large language models.

Current Role

Currently working as an MLE & Research Intern at TikTok Australia, focusing on MLLMs and related applied research.

Research Interests

  • Graph Mining: subgraph detection, fairness-aware mining, graph analytics
  • Knowledge Graph Reasoning: multi-hop reasoning, retrieval-augmented generation
  • Large Language Models: knowledge-enhanced reasoning, interpretability
